TIPS FOR TOURISTS :

Safety Tips for Foreigners, Tourists and Visitors to Goa

The Goa Police Department would like to make your visit to our beautiful State as pleasant and safe as possible. You can help us further by following these simple tips while travelling around Goa.

  • Carry your wallet in an inside jacket pocket or side trouser pocket, never in the rear pocket.
  • Carry pocketbooks in the bend of your elbow, held close to your body.
  • Keep pocketbooks on your lap when dining out, not dangling over the back of a chair.
  • Leave valuables and important papers, such as jewelry and passports, secure in your hotel's safe deposit box.
  • Be wary of unexpected persons coming to your hotel room.
  • If you schedule a meeting with a potential client, research the company and meet in a public place.
  • Make sure luggage is only given to hotel bell staff and take a receipt for stored luggage.
  • Public smoking is an offence in Goa. Smoking is permitted only in designated smoking zones.
  • Consumption of alcohol is not permitted at Public places.
  • No littering, spitting in public places, beaches, parks and public transport.
  • Always throw litter in the dustbin.
  • Cooking in the open places is not advisable.
  • No urination or defecation in public is permitted.
  • Loud music in open areas after 10:00 p.m. is not permitted.
  • Tourists must wear helmets while riding two-wheelers.
  • Do not use mobile phones while driving.
  • Park your vehicle only at designated parking places.
  • Wear seat belt while driving four wheelers. Always follow lane driving.
  • Use of plastics is banned in Goa. Any violation may invite penalty.
  • Beware of inducement of touts, always do business with authorized persons/agents.
  • Do not buy or sell drugs, you may land into trouble. Provisions of NDPS Act are very stringent.
  • No squatting is allowed on footpaths. You may be prosecuted for causing obstruction.
  • Respect local customs and traditions in the interest of public Harmony.
  • Eve-teasing or stalking may invite legal trouble.
  • It is indecent to dry your clothes in public places. Apart from risk of being stolen, it interferes with overall ambience of the place.
  • Do not venture into sea during night time especially after consumption of alcohol.
  • Always follow instructions of ‘Drishti life guards' and Tourist Police with regard to designated bathing zone.
  • In the event of any dispute with taxi operator, tour operators, do not indulge in quarrel. Instead inform Police Control Room (100)
  • In the event of any medical emergency dial 108.
  • Do not drink on beaches as it is banned and punishable with fine is found breaking empty glass bottles and throwing on the beach.
  • Do not encourage beggars. Begging is offence in Goa.
  • In case you see drug peddler, please inform nearest Police Station. Information given will be kept confidential. Trafficking and possession of drugs narcotics is a very serious offence.
  • Do not indulge in permissive sex as you run risk of being afflicted by disease of AIDS.
  • Treat children with respect. Paedophilia is an offence pinishable under the law in Goa. Do not indulge in pardophilia.
  • Keep your valuables in hotel lockers/bank's deposit vaults.
  • Exhibitionism should be avoided in residential areas.
  • Obeserve decency and decorum in dress when visiting religios places such as Temples,Churches, Mosque etc., Nudity on beaches and public places is forbidden and punishable under the law.
  • Do not leave your belongings unattended.
  • Do not drive any vehicle on the beach. It is prohibited.
  • For additional information with regards to tourism in Goa, visit the Goa Toursim website at http://www.goatourism.gov.in
