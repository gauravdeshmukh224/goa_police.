Purse Snatching
  • Do NOT carry a bag that makes you a target. A bag dangling from the shoulder can be easily yanked off.
  • Do NOT carry your bag in a way that you cannot let go. Many women are injured while resisting.
  • Be aware of your surroundings and carry your bag close to your body, tucked in the bend of your elbow.
  • Minimize the money and credit cards you carry. Divide money between pockets and bags.
  • If you are a victim, do not fight to hold onto your bag, especially if a weapon is involved.
At Home You Should
  • Keep your key ready before reaching the door.
  • Ensure your entrance area is well lit.
  • Do not hold the lobby door open for strangers.
  • Use the door chain before fully opening the door.
  • List only your last name and first initial on your mailbox.
  • If a stranger wants to use your phone, keep them outside and make the call yourself.
  • If you find your door open, DO NOT enter. Call police from a neighbor's house.
Coming Home Late at Night
  • Avoid shortcuts that are poorly lit or isolated.
  • Know which stores are open late in your neighborhood.
  • If you suspect someone is following you, head toward a busy open place.
  • Keep your keys in your hand until safely inside.
  • If dropped by auto, ask the driver to wait until you are inside safely.
  • If a motorist bothers you, walk in the opposite direction repeatedly.
If You Are Driving
  • Keep windows rolled up and doors locked.
  • If forced off the road, blow your horn continuously to attract attention.
  • If stopped, put your car in reverse and back away if possible.
  • If followed, make turns through active streets.
  • Head to the nearest police station, fire station, or open store if threatened.
  • Park in well-lit areas and check surroundings before exiting.
  • Check back seat before entering and lock car when leaving.
  • Use a good electronic anti-theft lock.
