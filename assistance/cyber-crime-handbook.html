HANDBOOK ON RECENT COMMON METHODS ADOPTED BY CRIMINALS TO CHEAT

Phone Call Bank Fraud

Fraudsters call pretending to be bank officials claiming debit card is blocked/expired and ask for CVV, OTP and PIN. After receiving details they make online or POS transactions.

Credit Card Reward Scam

Fraudsters claim reward points are expiring and ask for card details to redeem benefits. Victim shares details and loses money.

Treasure Scam

Fraudsters lure victims with stories of hidden gold or precious stones and demand advance money for travel or documentation.

Social Media Friendship Scam

Fake profiles of wealthy individuals develop friendship and later demand money for taxes, customs, gifts, business deals or emergencies.

Gift Parcel Scam

Victim receives call from fake customs officer demanding payment to release parcel containing foreign currency and expensive gifts.

Lottery / Email Scam

Messages claiming lottery winnings demand advance tax or processing fees.

Marriage Site Scam

Fake marriage profiles gain trust and request financial help due to fake emergencies or business losses.

Online Job Fraud

Fake job offers with attractive salaries demand payment for interviews, visa or travel processing. Victims lose huge amounts.

Website / OLX Fraud

Fraud websites sell products at very cheap rates. Victims pay in advance but never receive goods.

Wealthy Heirs Scam

Fraudsters promise large inheritance money and demand payment for IMF, UN or tax clearance charges.

Income Tax / Insurance Refund Scam

Fraudsters promise tax refund or insurance maturity benefits and demand advance tax payments.

Cheap Loan Scam

Fraud advertisements offer loans at low interest with no surety and demand processing fees in advance.

Caution

If you come across any of these situations, do not share bank details or deposit money into private accounts. Be alert and aware.
