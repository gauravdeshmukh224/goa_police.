DRUGS AND YOU

Features of The Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985

Key Features of NDPS Act, 1985

  1. All offences booked under this Act are tried in specially designated NDPS Courts with a Judge having the rank of Sessions Judge.
  2. All offences under NDPS Act are of non-bailable nature.
  3. Very strict punishment ranging from 6 months minimum to maximum 30 years depending on the nature of offence.
  4. Provision for death penalty for certain offences after previous convictions under NDPS Act.
  5. Provision for preventive detention.
  6. Provision for forfeiture of property.
  7. Cases are registered and investigated by officers of and above the rank of Police Sub-Inspector.
  8. Fast disposal of cases by NDPS Court.

DOs

  1. Inform the Police if you observe drug consumption or trafficking.
  2. Carry doctor’s prescription if you are under medication.
  3. Buy drugs only from an authorised and licensed pharmacy and obtain a cash memo.
  4. Respect the laws of the land in which you are touring.

DON'Ts

  1. Do not indulge in any act that may attract punishment under NDPS Act.
  2. Do not encourage touts and dubious persons in befriending you.
  3. Do not frequent bars and restaurants suspected of encouraging drug consumption.
  4. Do not buy any drug without prescription and from unauthorised pharmacies.
  5. Do not allow strangers or casual acquaintances to stay in your room.
  6. Do not attend dubious parties such as acid parties.
  7. Do not experiment with any foreign substances whether orally or intravenously.
